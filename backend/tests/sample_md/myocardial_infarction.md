# Myocardial Infarction

Myocardial infarction (MI), commonly known as a heart attack, occurs when
blood flow to part of the heart muscle is blocked, typically by a
thrombus on a ruptured atherosclerotic plaque in a coronary artery.

## Symptoms

Classic symptoms include crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm
or jaw, shortness of breath, diaphoresis, and nausea. ST-elevation on
ECG indicates a STEMI.

## Treatment

Acute treatment includes aspirin, clopidogrel, heparin, and emergency
percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI). Long-term management includes
beta-blockers, ACE inhibitors, and statins.
